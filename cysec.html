CYSEC

Cyber Security • iAM Scholar

Ethical hacking, network defense, cryptography, and digital forensics

Course overview: This course introduces students to the core principles of cyber security, including threat analysis, ethical hacking, cryptography, and digital forensics. Designed for aspiring security analysts, penetration testers, and ethical hackers.

Chapter 1 — Introduction to Cyber Security

Understand the landscape of digital threats and the importance of security.
Notes:
  • Cyber security is the practice of protecting systems, networks, and programs from digital attacks.
  • Common threats include malware, phishing, ransomware, and social engineering.
  • Security goals: Confidentiality, Integrity, Availability (CIA Triad).
  • Types of hackers: White hat (ethical), black hat (malicious), grey hat (mixed).
  • Security domains: Network, application, endpoint, cloud, and data security.

Chapter 2 — Network Security Fundamentals

Explore how networks are protected from unauthorized access and attacks.
Notes:
  • Firewalls filter traffic based on rules; IDS/IPS detect and prevent intrusions.
  • Common protocols: TCP/IP, DNS, DHCP, HTTP/S, FTP, SSH.
  • Network segmentation and VLANs improve isolation and control.
  • VPNs encrypt traffic between endpoints over public networks.
  • MAC filtering, port security, and access control lists (ACLs) enhance protection.

Chapter 3 — Ethical Hacking & Penetration Testing

Learn how ethical hackers simulate attacks to find vulnerabilities.
Notes:
  • Penetration testing involves reconnaissance, scanning, exploitation, and reporting.
  • Tools: Nmap (network mapper), Metasploit (exploit framework), Burp Suite (web testing).
  • Reconnaissance: Passive (WHOIS, Google dorking) vs active (ping, port scans).
  • Vulnerability scanning identifies weaknesses before exploitation.
  • Reporting includes risk rating, remediation steps, and executive summaries.

Chapter 4 — Cryptography

Understand how encryption protects data and ensures integrity.
Notes:
  • Cryptography secures communication through encoding and decoding data.
  • Types: Symmetric (AES, DES) and Asymmetric (RSA, ECC).
  • Hashing algorithms: MD5, SHA-1, SHA-256 (used for integrity checks).
  • Digital signatures verify authenticity and non-repudiation.
  • SSL/TLS protocols secure web traffic; certificates validate identity.

Chapter 5 — Security Policies & Risk Management

Learn how organizations manage risks and enforce security policies.
Notes:
  • Security policies define acceptable use, access control, and incident response.
  • Risk = Threat × Vulnerability × Impact.
  • Risk mitigation strategies: Avoidance, reduction, transfer, acceptance.
  • Compliance standards: ISO 27001, NIST, GDPR, HIPAA.
  • Security awareness training reduces human error and insider threats.

Chapter 6 — Digital Forensics & Incident Response

Investigate breaches and respond to security incidents effectively.
Notes:
  • Digital forensics involves collecting, analyzing, and preserving evidence.
  • Chain of custody ensures integrity of evidence during investigation.
  • Tools: Autopsy, FTK, EnCase, Volatility (memory analysis).
  • Incident response phases: Preparation, detection, containment, eradication, recovery, lessons learned.
  • Log analysis, SIEM systems, and threat intelligence aid investigations.
